What is JORE?

The Journal Of Research Experiences is an experimental zine & journal run by graduate students, for graduate students. We collect media about all aspects of academia and seek to be a truthful, useful, and entertaining resource for aspiring academics, where researchers are free to get into the nitty gritty and personal side of their work. We accept formal articles, free form zine musings, poetry, and standalone "figures".

JORE is currently in its alpha testing phase. We’re thinking about doing sporadic (~2 or 3 months) releases of our issues, each with a loose theme.

Call for Submissions

Submission Deadline: June 1 (though we all know none of you have any executive function and this is going to be extended to June 5).

Prospective authors are invited to submit low quality papers, standalone figures, and content not limited to but including recaps, nonfiction poetry, and rants to the July 2024 edition of JORE.

The theme this month is “Failed Experiments”. Practically every researcher has experienced attempting an experiment that did not pan out, and was not included in publication due to a lack of results. This is an opportunity to expound on things you’ve tried that never made it to publication, whether that be initial iterations not worthy of mention or a completely scrapped project. Entertaining photos or early prototypes, if relevant, are encouraged, as are dives into the background and planning around a project. “Failed experiments” is a broad umbrella– maybe your lab or class tried something new (lab lunch before the meeting) that resulted in unintended consequences.

As always, we also welcome off-theme submissions.
